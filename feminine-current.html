This is a day for women who are holding a great deal, professionally, personally, and emotionally, and who are ready to pause long enough to hear themselves again.

What this is, and what it is not

What this is not

Not a conference.

Not a workshop.

Not a networking event.

What this is

A carefully curated day designed to give back the kind of space that leadership rarely allows.

A day to step out of the noise of doing, and turn quietly toward who you are now.

Who this day is for

For women navigating what comes next.

The Feminine Current is created for women over 40 navigating leadership, transition, ambition, identity, and what comes next.

You may be questioning the version of success you have been holding. You may be tired in a way rest does not seem to fix. You may be sensing that the next chapter wants something different from you, but unable to slow down long enough to listen.

You do not need to arrive with answers. Only with a willingness to pause.

What the day holds

A day shaped to give back the space leadership rarely allows.

Through guided reflection, meaningful conversation, coaching experiences, nervous system support, movement, and inspiring guest voices, we explore leadership beyond performance and success beyond burnout.

The day moves through five quiet movements.

  1. 01

    The Exhale

    A gentle arrival. Breakfast, grounding, no titles or introductions.

  2. 02

    The Leader I Became

    Reflection on what leadership has asked of you, and what it has shaped in you.

  3. 03

    What I Am Ready to Release

    The patterns, performances, and quiet costs that no longer serve.

  4. 04

    Redefining Success

    A featured conversation with a woman who reinvented herself at forty.

  5. 05

    The Woman I Am Becoming

    A forward-looking session and a personal compass for the next chapter.

The day closes with a nervous system rest, led by Barbara — a gentle return to yourself before stepping back into the world.

What you might leave with

Three quiet things you may carry home.

  • A clearer sense of where you are now.

  • A loosening of what has been held too tightly.

  • Permission to lead, and to live, in a way that feels more like you.

About Rowena

A space she wished existed.

Rowena Doyle is the founder of Reframd. She works with women in leadership who are ready to question the version of success they have been holding, and to recalibrate toward something more sustainable, aligned, and true.

The Feminine Current is the day she wished existed when she was inside a large organisation, looking for a space that understood the particular weight women in leadership carry.

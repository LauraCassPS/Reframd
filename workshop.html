About this workshop

Most career change advice starts with the job market. This one starts with you.

We begin where you actually are — not where you think you should be. From there, you'll audit your skills across four categories and turn that into a clear, concrete path forward. You'll leave with a completed worksheet and a quieter head.

What we'll cover

The session in three parts.

01

Honest A to B

We start with where you actually are. Then we get clear on B — not the polished version, the real one. Has A become more honest? Has B become clearer? Two minutes of quiet. Some things shift.

02

Skills Audit

Four categories. One question at the centre: what's serving where you're going? You'll work through Love, Drop, Learn, and Acquire on your worksheet. Ten minutes of focused, specific work.

03

Your Path Forward

The audit becomes a plan — quick wins this month, moves over the next 90 days, structural changes within a year. Professional and personal. Because the conditions that make a plan possible matter as much as the plan itself.

A few questions

Things people ask.

Is it really free? +

Yes. No catch. This is a workshop we're running because we want to — to get the tools into people's hands. You'll need nothing more than a device and an internet connection.

What do I need to join? +

A device and the Google Meet link we'll send you. You don't need a Google account to join as a participant. We'll also send your worksheet ahead of the session — print it or use it on screen.

Do I need to prepare anything? +

Nothing formal. Just bring yourself and an honest answer to this: where are you, and where do you want to be? That's your raw material. We'll do the rest together.

What if I don't know what B is yet? +

That's exactly why you should come. B doesn't need to be fully formed — it just needs to be honest. One of the things this session does is help you articulate it, even if it's still blurry. You'll leave with something clearer than you arrived with.

Who is this for? +

People who know something needs to change — a role, a field, a way of working — but haven't fully mapped it yet. Mid-career, mid-change. Not early career. This is for people who have something to audit.
